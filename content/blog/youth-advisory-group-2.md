What do teenagers think of when they hear the word 'agency'? What ways are teenagers using generative AI to support their learning? In what situations with generative AI do they believe their agency is threatened? These were some of the questions we were hoping to get the youth advisory group's input on when we met with them yesterday.

This session, we were wearing both our organiser and researcher hats. We held a vote on a draft code of conduct for young people and researchers, developed following their brainstorming in the kickoff meeting, before diving into an introduction to the CHAILD project.

Teenagers defined agency as *acting for yourself*, *making decisions*, and *having a say/control*. They also used terms like *independence* and *responsibility* when thinking about agency.

As part of our co-design study, we're planning to use storyboards of situations where generative AI systems don't support agency to understand how teenagers perceive and deal with this. We presented several initial ideas for the stories to the group in order to understand which they felt were relevant and relatable, as well as how we could improve the stories. The young people provided really helpful feedback and suggestions on this, highlighting how using generative AI can feel like a 'slippery slope'---despite an initial intention to use it only for ideas, they find themselves influenced by AI responses to use it more than they had intended.

As researchers, we're looking forward to returning to the group to show how we've used their guidance to shape our study design. As organisers, we're excited about the other projects seeking to consult with the youth advisory group, including projects developing a quantum technology outreach workshop and involving young people in reviewing citizen science projects.

The project is part of the [MPLS Public and Community Engagement with Research Fellowship](https://www.mpls.ox.ac.uk/public-engagement/pcer-fellows-scheme-25-26).
