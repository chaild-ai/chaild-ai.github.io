Now that the whirlwind of CHI 2026 has passed and the dust is beginning to settle, we wanted to share some reflections from our time at the conference.

CHI was huge: around 5,000 people attended over five days in Barcelona, from Monday 13 to Friday 17 April. Every day included paper sessions in the mornings and meetups and workshops in the afternoons, with breaks in between to see posters and interactive demos in the main hall. Relevant sessions often ran in different rooms at the same time, so Isobel and Vid split up whenever possible to cover more ground.

![The main exhibition hall at CHI 2026, crowded with attendees](/content/blog/images/chi-2026-main-hall.jpg "The main hall during a break")

Roughly two thirds of the presentations and posters were about AI, and about a third about VR (with some overlap between the two). If you are curious how the field fits together, [the topography of HCI](https://topography-of-hci.dk/) has a model of research areas crowdsourced from conference participants.

## The CAMCAD workshop

On Thursday 16 April, we hosted our workshop on [Child-Centred AI-Mediated Collaborative Agency by Design (CAMCAD)](https://oxfordhcc.github.io/CAMCAD/), continuing the series of Child-Centred AI workshops at CHI 2023 and 2024. The workshop's extended abstract is published in the [CHI 2026 Proceedings](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772363.3778754). Around 15 participants joined us, including workshop authors and guests.

![Workshop participants standing together in front of the CAMCAD title slide](/content/blog/images/chi-2026-camcad-group.jpg "CAMCAD workshop participants")

The first half of the workshop was dedicated to invited presentations. Professor Netta Iivari (University of Oulu) opened with a keynote on fostering transformative agency of children in the age of AI — a collective process of recognising injustice, imagining alternatives, and acting together, which she distilled into a challenge for all of us: "If you're designing for agency, you should be designing for collectives." Five invited talks followed: Dr Hawra Rabaan on enacting collaborative agency through critical AI literacy, Diana Sarai Hernandez Manzo on a virtual heritage VR escape room with AI agents, Keyu Mao on child–robot trust, Mennatullah Hendawy on moving child online safety from control to co-agency, and Yui Kondo on the Algorithmic Mirror.

In the break between sessions, we held a poster session where fourteen accepted posters and pictorials were presented.

![Three accepted workshop posters displayed on poster boards](/content/blog/images/chi-2026-poster-session.jpg "Posters at the CAMCAD poster session")

The second half was group discussion. Participants explored the risks AI tools pose to children's agency — from eroding trust in human relationships (commercial AI systems are designed to please, in ways real relationships cannot match) to a loss of creativity. The groups then worked on design responses: for example, LLMs that use the Socratic method and step-by-step guidance to keep decision-making with the child, and "pro-relational" designs that point children to a trusted adult — a teacher, parent, librarian, or peer — before offering their own take. A recurring thought was that agency is a skill: making and enacting decisions is how people get better at it.

All presentations were recorded — you can find the recordings, the accepted submissions, and the [full workshop report](https://oxfordhcc.github.io/CAMCAD/report/) on the workshop website.

## Beyond the workshop

Agency and autonomy came up many times across the conference (we were, of course, biased towards finding them). Two highlights: [Cocoa](https://programs.sigchi.org/chi/2026/program/content/223085), a Jupyter notebook extension for co-planning and co-execution with AI agents that encourages user input at multiple steps rather than one-shot prompt-and-output interaction (and won a best paper award), and [Does My Chatbot Have an Agenda?](https://programs.sigchi.org/chi/2026/program/content/222115), on understanding human and AI agency in chatbot interaction.

## What's next

Following the workshop, a call for papers is open for a special issue on Agency in Child-AI Interaction in the [International Journal of Child-Computer Interaction](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/international-journal-of-child-computer-interaction), covering agency broadly — not just collaborative agency. Several related workshops are also coming up at [IDC 2026](https://idc.acm.org/2026/attending/accepted-workshops/) in June, and the conversation continues on the [CAMCAD community Discord](https://discord.gg/6kuHZdetEJ).
