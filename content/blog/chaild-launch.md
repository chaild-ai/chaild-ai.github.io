We are delighted to announce the start of CHAILD — **Children's Agency In the age of AI: Leveraging InterDisciplinarity** — a research project funded by [UK Research and Innovation](https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=MR%2FZ505882%2F1) (ref. MR/Z505882/1).

AI is rapidly becoming part of children's everyday lives: in the tools they learn with, the platforms they socialise on, and the media they consume. CHAILD asks how we can make sure children remain *agents* in that world — able to make informed decisions and act on them meaningfully — rather than passive recipients of whatever AI systems serve them.

True to the "InterDisciplinarity" in our name, the project brings together computer scientists, philosophers, education researchers, and children's rights advocates from the University of Oxford's Department of Computer Science and Institute for Ethics in AI, the UCL Institute of Education's Knowledge Lab, and the 5Rights Foundation. You can meet the whole team on our homepage.

Over the coming years, we will be working on five missions:

- Defining children's critical agency in the digital context
- Establishing approaches to assess the impact on agency
- Developing design principles for agency
- Creating new guidelines for parental and educational support
- Formulating policy recommendations to prioritise children's agency

We will share our studies, publications, events, and opportunities to get involved right here — watch this space.
