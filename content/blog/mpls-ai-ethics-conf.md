On 15 January 2026, members of the CHAILD team attended the MPLS Researcher Conference: AI & Ethics, which brought together researchers across the University of Oxford's Mathematical, Physical, and Life Sciences division to discuss challenges with AI in research and society.

CHAILD researcher Leslye Dias Duran received a prize for her presentation on *How can AI support children's agency?*, with the judges appreciating a philosopher's perspective.

Isobel Voysey shared about the new youth advisory group, which received interest from several researchers looking to consult with the group, from departments including Chemistry and Astrophysics.

Additionally, Prof. Sir Nigel Shadbolt was involved in a stimulating roundtable discussion on responsible AI in science and the future of research careers.
